Brand Identity Brief
Practice Learning LLC  ·  St. Petersburg, Florida
"Quality is not a destination. It's a practice."
Why "Practice" — Four Meanings, One Name
  • i
    Practice as verb The act of doing, refining, and building skill through intentional repetition — the foundation of great instructional design.
  • ii
    Practice as profession A law practice. A medical practice. A consulting practice. Signals established expertise and gravitas.
  • iii
    Practice as philosophy Continuous improvement over certification. Quality is ongoing — not a box to check, but a way of working.
  • iv
    Practice as pedagogy Deliberate practice is the bridge between theory and application — the space where learning actually happens.
Brand Philosophy

Effective digital learning is never finished. It is practiced — designed with intention, refined through critique, and improved continuously. Practice Learning exists for the educators, designers, and institutions who understand that quality is not a credential you earn once. It is a standard you uphold every day.

Three Pillars
I

Rigorous Standards

Grounded in 14+ years at the forefront of digital course quality, with deep expertise in Quality Matters frameworks and what it truly takes to earn — and sustain — a mark of excellence.

II

Intentional Craft

Instructional design is both discipline and art. Every learning experience is built with purpose — informed by pedagogy, shaped by design thinking, and refined through the kind of critique that only comes from deep experience.

III

Forward Fluency

AI is reshaping how learning is designed and delivered. Practice Learning helps institutions and faculty integrate these tools thoughtfully — without losing the human practitioner at the center.

Brand Voice

Practice Learning Sounds Like

  • Confident without being prescriptive
  • Warm but substantive — never fluffy
  • Precise language, never jargon-heavy
  • The authority in the room who asks the best questions
  • Someone who has done this work, not just studied it
  • A practitioner who respects the craft of teaching

Practice Learning Never Sounds Like

  • A vendor pitching a platform or product
  • An academic paper with no practical application
  • A startup promising to "disrupt" education
  • Generic consulting speak ("leverage synergies")
  • Uncertain or deferential about quality standards
  • Someone chasing certifications rather than growth
